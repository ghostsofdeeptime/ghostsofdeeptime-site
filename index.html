Why I Wrote This Book

I didn’t plan to write this book — it arrived like a half-remembered story. Ghosts of Deep Time began with a question that wouldn’t leave me: What if there was a civilisation before everything we’ve ever called civilisation?

The traces wouldn’t be monuments. They’d be quieter: a curve in a stone, a shape in a story, a dream that keeps returning.

What Is Deep Time — And Why Might It Remember?

Deep time is not just old time. It’s a silence so long it becomes something else.

It stretches across millions of years, where memory has no page, and history has no pen. But maybe, just maybe, something remembers — in the curve of a river, the shape of a tool, the pulse of a myth.

Was There a Civilisation Before History?

We tell ourselves that history began with writing — with tablets and temples and kings. But what if that’s not where the real story starts?

Long before the Sumerians and the pyramids, before we etched meaning into stone, there were beings who stood and watched the sky. Hominids. Humans, almost. Or maybe just human enough to remember.

Books Like Graham Hancock — But With Poetry Instead of Proof

Some books try to rewrite history. Others just invite you to remember what it forgot.

If you’ve read Graham Hancock or watched Ancient Apocalypse, you’ve probably felt it — that pull toward a deeper story. A missing chapter beneath the ruins. A whisper that says: Something happened here.

What if there was a civilisation before ours — not built of stone, but of story?

The Memory Beneath the Fossil

The fossil is not the whole story. It’s the outline of something deeper — a breath, a moment, a life lived in rhythm with the world. What’s missing from the fossil is where the real mystery lives.

Q&A

Why did you write this?
Because the idea wouldn’t leave me. A whisper I had to follow.

Is this fiction or fact?
Neither. It’s a memory exercise for the species.

What is “deep time” to you?
Time beyond clocks — something geological, mythic, lived.

Can memory survive without writing?
Yes. In dreams, instincts, rituals — even in bone.

Do you believe a civilisation existed 2 million years ago?
I believe something happened. Something shaped us. It left no cities — only echoes.

What kind of civilisation was it?
Not one of machines or power. One of presence. Of resonance. Of memory before words.

Are myths just stories?
No. They’re mnemonic echoes — patterns remembered in motion.

So this is all metaphor?
It’s metaphor and memory, wrapped together like bone and symbol.

What do you want readers to do with this?
Remember. Not fact-check. Feel-check. Ask again.

Is there a next step?
This book is the first echo. You are the second.
