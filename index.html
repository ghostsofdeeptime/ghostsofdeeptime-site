Exploring Lost Civilisations Before History

Ghosts of Deep Time invites you to imagine a lost civilisation—one that may have walked the Earth between 2 million and 500,000 years ago—and asks what traces might still live in our bodies, stories, and dreams.

This is not a theory. It’s a meditation. A question. A ghost-story rooted in geology, myth, and evolutionary memory—the kind of story that doesn’t rewrite history, but quietly reframes how we remember it.

Reflections from Deep Time

Why I Wrote This Book

I didn’t plan to write this book—it arrived like a half-remembered story. Ghosts of Deep Time began with a question that wouldn’t leave me: What if there was a civilisation before everything we’ve ever called civilisation?

The traces wouldn’t be monuments. They’d be quieter: a curve in a stone, a shape in a story, a dream that keeps returning.

What Is Deep Time—And Why Might It Remember?

Deep time stretches across millions of years, where memory has no page and history has no pen. But maybe, just maybe, something remembers—in the curve of a river, the shape of a tool, the pulse of a myth.

Was There a Civilisation Before History?

We tell ourselves that history began with writing—with tablets and temples and kings. But what if that’s not where the real story starts? Long before the Sumerians and pyramids, perhaps beings watched the sky and passed down whispers in stone.

Books Like Graham Hancock—But With Poetry Instead of Proof

Some books try to rewrite history. Others just invite you to remember what it forgot.

What if there was a civilisation before ours—not built of stone, but of story?

The Memory Beneath the Fossil

The fossil is not the whole story. It’s the outline of something deeper—a breath, a moment, a life lived in rhythm with the world. What’s missing from the fossil is where the real mystery lives.

Questions & Answers

What inspired you to write Ghosts of Deep Time? I kept seeing traces that didn’t fit. Stories that lingered longer than they should.

Why is it poetic? Because memory needs feeling more than facts.

Why are sections so short? Like flashes, not lectures—they invite rather than explain.

Why take it seriously? Imagination and science both seek deeper truths.

How structured? A spiral—enter anywhere and feel the shape.

Are these just metaphors? Not exactly—some readers feel a pulse behind the words.

Inspired by real finds? Yes—and by the silences and gaps in the record.

Imagination vs speculation? This is an offering, not a claim—it blends myth & possibility.

Who is this for? Anyone who feels history missing something, who prefers wonder over proof.

Evidence for a pre-human civ? Clues and anomalies—but this book offers the feeling, not the theory.

Another Atlantis? No—this is older, quieter, not bound to tech or temples.

Next step? Pause. Sit. Listen. Let it begin when you close the book.

Sequel? Where Echo Lives—listening for ghosts of the present moment.

Support? Share, review, whisper the story to someone who needs it.

Who is Pedro Malha? A listener, a father, someone trying to remember something older than himself.

How was it written? Over years, in fragments, in quiet moments.

Self-published? Yes—freedom to breathe.

Contact? pedro@ghostsofdeeptime.com.
